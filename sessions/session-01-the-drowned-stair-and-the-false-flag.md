# Session 1 — The Drowned Stair and the False Flag

**In-world span:** IR 1187, **6th–14th of Highsun** (~9 days), early-mid summer.
**Ends:** Selvecross docks, late afternoon of the 14th — on a cliffhanger.
**Current date going forward:** **15th of Highsun, IR 1187.**

---

## Previously…

Paige stepped off Dalia Renn's caravan into the mud and smoke of **Selvecross** a penniless
drifter, and nine days later came back up the river standing in a **dead man's boat** — four
bound raiders behind her, a freed bargeman beside her, and a strongbox of imperial secrets under
her cloak. Between the two arrivals she read the wards of a **drowned elder ruin** and found a
living thing chained in the dark beneath it; struck a **bargain** with that thing for power that
moves earth and stone; and ran a band of "river-raiders" to ground in a creek-pinch up the Wend —
only to learn they were no raiders at all, but **settlers paid in fresh imperial coin** to play
the part, murder under a false flag, and **blame the forest-folk** so a frightened town would beg
the Empire for soldiers. Now Paige stands on the Wharfstairs with the proof of it open in her
hands, the crowd murmuring, and **Hald Brennar's man walking up the dock** to bring her in. The
forearm wound aches. The coin is marked. Two raiders are loose in the woods who saw what her hands
can do. And somewhere down the river is "**the advisor**" whose closed-eye seal she does not yet
know she has seen twice.

---

## Key Events

- **Arrival & first footing.** Paige reached Selvecross off Dalia Renn's caravan, bought supplies,
  took cheap lodging near the **Drowned Crow**, and hunted work.
- **The surveyor's job.** Met imperial surveyor **Coll Verrane** (at the Ferryman's Rest), who
  needed a scholar to read elder ruin-marks. Paige took the work and joined his small upriver party.
- **The Drowned Stair.** ~1.5 days up the **Wend** to a half-submerged elder ruin (the **Sunken
  Gate**). Paige read the marks but deliberately delivered a **false reading** — skipping
  load-bearing marks, mangling the joins — so the worked **magic seal** would not loosen. In the
  reading she discovered the ruin is a **prison/threshold** holding an **ancient, sentient, powerful
  entity**, whose mind made direct contact. Paige feigned scholarly curiosity to learn what it was
  and what it wanted, then pulled back and warned Verrane "**something tried to possess me — this
  place is dangerous.**" She handed him a partial reading, **demanded and got triple the agreed
  pay**, and parted from the expedition.
- **The return, alone.** That night Paige secretly resolved to go back. She re-supplied, ran back up
  the Wend, and **practiced magic the whole way.**
- **The Pact.** Alone at the Drowned Stair, Paige meditated, conversed with the imprisoned entity,
  and struck a **pact**, eyes open: *she keeps control; it lends power; in exchange she grants it
  freedom over time and weighs its counsel fairly.* She understood the entity agreed readily because
  none of her terms truly blocked what it wanted — patience and eventual freedom — and made the deal
  anyway, meaning to outplay it. The pact **dramatically enhanced her magic, the earth-and-stone
  aspect most of all.**
- **Magic growth.** Paige drilled the new powers in the woods — combat, defense, utility — twice
  pushing herself near **Spent**.
- **The raider bounty.** Back in town she took a bounty off the Drowned Crow board (posted by
  **Borric Tann**, **backed by Hald Brennar** for the river-merchants) to stop the raiders preying on
  barges.
- **The hunt at Kettle Narrows.** On foot up a side-creek off the Wend, Paige used **earth-sense** and
  tracking to find the camp, **freed a captive bargeman, Garran**, and isolated and interrogated a lone
  raider — clamping hardened earth over his mouth.
- **The revelation.** The "raiders" are **settlers in disguise, paid by the Empire** to stage raids,
  **mark their kills to read as Wendfolk work**, and frighten Selvecross into begging for a garrison —
  a manufactured pretext.
- **The fight.** Paige set a **kill-zone** at the creek pinch: stones at range, **felled trees** onto
  the band, survivors **sunk waist-deep in hardened earth**. She **killed two** (the sentry, who cut
  her left forearm in a duel; one man crushed under a felled oak), **two escaped into the dark**, and
  **captured the boss, Calder** (an ex-imperial officer), alive and wounded.
- **The loot.** Loose silver; an **imperial-issue blade with the maker's stamp filed off**; a satchel
  of command-tent papers; a **barge-schedule ledger** proving a **town informer leaked the wharfmaster's
  records**; and a **locked iron-banded strongbox.** She left the bulky stolen cargo.
- **The return by boat.** Paige loaded four prisoners (incl. Calder), Garran, and all the evidence into
  the raiders' boat and ran ~1.5 days down the Wend and Selve into Selvecross, landing at the docks late
  afternoon to a gathering crowd.
- **The strongbox (opened under cloak; key from Calder's neck).** Inside:
  - A sewn purse of **fresh imperial-mint crowns** with a **quartermaster's tally-string** — a *wage*,
    not loot. **Marked, traceable coin.**
  - **Three unsigned officer's letters:** **(a)** a target list by date, ordering the dead **marked to
    read as Wendfolk work**; **(b)** payment terms — the balance paid "**when the Council sends to the
    river-fort for soldiers**" (paid for the *outcome*); **(c)** "**report only to the advisor; the
    survey office is not to be troubled with this**," closed with an **unfamiliar wax sigil — a closed
    eye over a vertical line.**
  - A slip in a hastier hand: barge names/dates matching the wharfmaster's records, ending "**— to R.,
    at the Crow, same as before.**" *(The informer's initial is **R.**; they drink at the Drowned Crow.)*
