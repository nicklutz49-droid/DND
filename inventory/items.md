## Notable & Personal Items
*(Keepsakes, mysterious finds, quest items, anything with story weight.)*
- **The commonplace-book ("Paige")** — a fat, battered, dog-eared journal: years of scavenged notes, diagrams, half-translated rites, pressed leaves and stone-rubbings. At once Paige's scholarship, their spellcraft, and their magical **focus**. Losing it would hurt.
- **Keepsakes of discarded lives** — a small handful of mismatched tokens kept from names since shed: a signet that isn't theirs, a child's carved animal, a faded ribbon. Each a story Paige doesn't always tell.

### The Raider Evidence *(taken at Kettle Narrows, 13th–14th of Highsun, IR 1187)*
> A bundle of damning proof that the river-raids were a staged imperial frame-up. **Load-bearing —
> Paige must decide at the docks what to reveal and what to keep hidden.**
- **Imperial-issue blade (maker's stamp filed off)** — a soldier's sword whose mark was deliberately
  scoured away to hide its origin. Quiet proof the "raiders" were no forest-folk.
- **Satchel of command-tent papers** — the band's working documents and correspondence.
- **Barge-schedule ledger** — sailing schedules that match the **Selvecross wharfmaster's records**:
  proof someone with access to those records **leaked them** to the raiders (a town informer).
- **Three unsigned letters** *(officer's hand)* — found in the strongbox: **(a)** a target list by date,
  ordering the dead **marked to read as Wendfolk work**; **(b)** payment terms — the balance to be paid
  "**when the Council sends to the river-fort for soldiers**" (paid for the *outcome*); **(c)** an
  instruction to "**report only to the advisor; the survey office is not to be troubled with this**,"
  closed with an **unfamiliar wax sigil: a closed eye over a vertical line.**
- **The informer's slip** *(different, hastier hand)* — barge names/dates matching the wharfmaster's
  records, ending "**— to R., at the Crow, same as before.**" *(The informer's initial is **R.**; they
  drink at the Drowned Crow.)*
- **Iron-banded strongbox + key** — the locked box the letters and wage-purse came in; key was on a cord
  around **Calder**'s neck. (The wage-purse itself is logged under `currency-and-assets.md` — marked,
  traceable imperial coin.)
