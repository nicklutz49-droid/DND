### Selvecross
- **Type:** Settler river-town — the Selvemark's hub and great crossing; booming, half-lawless,
  built of green timber and mud.
- **Where:** At the **fork where the Wend** (a fast, narrow tributary out of the northern hills)
  **pours into the Selve.** A long gravel bar and the shallow back-channel here make the **only
  reliable ford for many days' travel** in either direction — so every barge, raft, and forest
  trail funnels through it. It sits roughly mid-river on the Selve's middle reaches; up the Wend
  lies hill country and ruins, down the Selve lies the wider world.
- **Control / leans toward:** **Contested, settler-dominated, independent in practice.** Run by an
  uneasy **Crossing Council** of the men and women who own the wharves, the ferry, and the fur
  trade — but the real muscle belongs to **Hald Brennar**, the timber-and-ferry boss whose people
  keep what passes for order. Native forest folk trade at the town's edges and on the south bank
  but are not always made welcome inside the palisade. The **Empire has just reached in**: a
  chartered post, a surveyor, the first agents — a toehold, not yet a grip. No faction owns the
  town; all of them push.
- **Population & prosperity:** Perhaps **eight hundred** souls hard-settled, swelling past a
  thousand in trade-season with rivermen, trappers, and drifters. **Cash-rich and rough** — coin
  moves fast on furs, timber, and the toll every crossing pays. Prices gouge; lodging is scarce;
  no wall keeps out the woods entirely.
- **Known for / needs / fears:** *Known for* — the crossing toll, the great fur-and-timber market,
  and being the last "real" town before the deep woods. *Needs* — labor, guards, boatmen, anyone
  who can read a charter or treat with the tribes; food in late winter. *Fears* — fire in the
  timber-yards, a hard tribal turn against the settlers, and the slow shadow of the Empire's
  charter over its free trade.
- **Player's influence or holdings here:** None.
- **Open hooks:**
  - **Odd jobs at the Drowned Crow.** Borric the fixer always has work nailed up — caravan guard,
    debt-collecting, ruin-running, a barge short a hand. A penniless newcomer's fastest coin.
  - **The surveyor's strange questions.** Imperial man **Coll Verrane** pays well — but he wants
    guides and copyists for the *old stone in the hills*, not farmland or fur. He's asked after
    anyone who can read elder-marks. (A scholar like Paige is exactly what he's hunting for.)
  - **The crossing is a chokepoint everyone covets.** Brennar's grip, the Council's purse, the
    tribes' resentment, and the Empire's charter are all squeezing the same ford. Something gives
    this summer — and a capable newcomer could end up working for any side, or playing them.

**Named places & faces a newcomer meets in Selvecross:**
- **The Wharfstairs** — the docks and caravan-yard on the Selve's north bank: a chaos of jetties,
  the toll-house, fur-presses, ox-pens, and the muddy yard where settler caravans break and barges
  land. Where Paige and her caravan arrive.
- **The Drowned Crow** — the big riverside inn and taproom by the Wharfstairs: smoke, spruce-beer,
  pallets in the loft for a coin, and the board where work changes hands. The newcomer's hub.
- **Hald Brennar** — the town's strongman boss; owns the ferry, the biggest timber-crews, and the
  hard men who keep order. Genial, immovable, and watching everything that lands.
- **Mother Sable** — keeper of the Drowned Crow; missing two fingers, forgets nothing, knows every
  face on the river. Trades gossip as freely as beer.
- **Borric Tann** — the Crow's work-broker and fixer; a quick, ferret-faced settler who deals out
  odd jobs for a cut and knows who needs what done quietly.
- **Coll Verrane** — the visible face of the Empire here: a soft-spoken imperial **surveyor** with a
  charter, good coin, and an unusual hunger for the elder ruins and anyone who can read them.

## Landmarks, Wilds & Ruins
*(Natural features, roads, rivers, and known **ruins of the elder age**. What built the
ruins is not commonly known — see `dm-secrets/`.)*

- **The river Selve** — the great river and only true highway of the Selvemark. Wide, brown, and
  slow on its middle reaches; everything moves on it by barge, raft, and canoe. Freezes hard in
  winter and runs dangerous in mud-season.
- **The Wend** — a fast, narrow tributary spilling out of the northern hills into the Selve at
  Selvecross. Canoe-navigable a way upstream toward hill country; the route trappers and
  ruin-hunters take into the back-country.
- **The Crossing (the Selvecross ford)** — the gravel bar and shallow back-channel where the Wend
  meets the Selve: the only reliable ford and ferry for many days in either direction. The reason
  the town exists; the chokepoint all traffic pays to pass.
- **Elder ruins of the Selvemark** — moss-grown stone of a **vanished elder people** lies scattered
  through the surrounding forest, thicker up the Wend in the hills. Common knowledge that they
  exist and that trappers stumble on them; **what built them, or why they fell, no one can say.**
  They draw ruin-hunters and scholars — and, lately, quiet imperial interest.
