# Factions & Reputation

> Every faction the player knows of, the player's **standing** with each, and the shifting
> **relationships** between them. The chronicler updates standings as they change in play;
> the world-simulator moves factions between sessions. Only what the player actually knows
> belongs here — secret agendas live in `dm-secrets/hidden-npcs.md`, the Empire's true
> design in `dm-secrets/empire-metaplot.md`.

## Standing Scale
A faction's disposition toward the player sits on this band (always note *why* it moved):

`Reviled · Hostile · Distrusted · Neutral / Unknown · Accepted · Friendly · Trusted · Honored · Revered`

> A newcomer starts at **Neutral / Unknown** with nearly everyone.

## The Three Powers (frontier-wide)
The broad forces in motion — **not monoliths.** Specific tribes, imperial offices, and
settler companies get their own blocks below as the player meets them. (Full detail in
`world/world-bible.md` §4.)

### The Empire of Acente
- **What it is:** The human-founded imperial state — bureaucracy, legions, magistrates,
  roads, taxes — expanding to absorb the frontier.
- **Player standing:** Neutral / Unknown *(officially)* — **but privately, quietly adversarial.**
- **Notes:** Licenses and controls magic; venerates an imperial pantheon; treats the elf &
  dwarf nations of Acente as independent rivals.
- **What Paige has learned (14th of Highsun, IR 1187):** Paige now holds **documentary proof**
  that the "river raiders" preying on Selvecross barges were **settlers in disguise, paid in fresh
  imperial coin** to stage attacks, mark their kills to read as **Wendfolk** work, and so frighten
  the Crossing Council into **begging the Empire for a garrison** — a manufactured pretext. The band's
  captured boss, an ex-imperial officer (**Calder**), reported "only to **the advisor**"; the orders
  bore an **unfamiliar wax sigil — a closed eye over a vertical line** — and warned that "**the survey
  office is not to be troubled with this**." Paige has, in effect, intercepted a piece of an imperial
  dirty operation. No one in the Empire yet knows she holds it — but **two raiders escaped** who saw
  her work outlawed magic.

### The Native Peoples
- **What it is:** The frontier's first peoples — great nations (goblin kingdoms, dragonborn
  city-states) and countless decentralized tribes of many kinds. They feud, ally, and trade
  among themselves.
- **Player standing:** Neutral / Unknown
- **Notes:** Each specific tribe/nation tracked separately as met. See **The Wendfolk** below —
  Paige has not met them, but has unknowingly done them a great service.

### The Rival Settlers
- **What it is:** Independent colonists, chartered companies, and opportunists racing to
  stake claims before the Empire swallows the frontier.
- **Player standing:** Neutral / Unknown
- **Notes:** Specific companies and settlements tracked separately as met.

### The Selvecross River-Merchants & Crossing Council
- **Type / power bloc:** Settler group — the wharf-, ferry-, and fur-owners who run the crossing,
  with **Hald Brennar**'s men for muscle.
- **Seat / territory:** Selvecross and the river trade up the Selve and Wend.
- **Leader(s):** The **Crossing Council** (collective); **Hald Brennar** holds the real muscle.
  **Borric Tann** brokers the work-board at the Drowned Crow.
- **Player standing:** **Accepted, and rising** — from unknown newcomer toward respected.
- **Disposition & why:** Paige took and **delivered** the river-raider bounty — broke the band,
  freed a captive bargeman, saved cargo from being lost, and hauled the raiders into town alive.
  As the session closed, a crowd was gathering at the docks and word racing to Brennar. The town
  is about to learn her name as "the one who brought the raiders in."
- **Goals:** Keep the crossing's trade flowing and free; protect barges; keep order on the river.
- **Relations with other factions:** Wary of the **Empire's** charter creeping over their free
  trade; uneasy with the **Wendfolk** at the palisade's edge; the crossing is a chokepoint every
  power covets.
- **Open threads:** The **bounty is owed but not yet paid** (Borric / the merchants). Paige holds
  evidence that the raids were a frame-up — and that **a town informer ("R.," who drinks at the
  Drowned Crow) leaked the wharfmaster's barge schedules.** How much she reveals to Brennar's man
  at the dock, and to whom, decides how the town turns.

### The Wendfolk (native forest-folk of the Wend)
- **Type / power bloc:** Native peoples — the forest-folk of the Wend country up off the river.
- **Seat / territory:** The deep woods and hill country up the Wend; they trade at Selvecross'
  edges and on the south bank, not always made welcome inside the palisade.
- **Leader(s):** Unknown to Paige.
- **Player standing:** **Neutral / Unknown** — *unmet,* but with **latent, unspoken goodwill earned.**
- **Disposition & why:** They have no idea Paige exists — but she **uncovered and foiled** a plot to
  pin the river-raids on them and brand them the aggressors. Whether they ever learn of it, she has
  quietly kept a war off their heads.
- **Goals:** Unknown to Paige — to hold their woods and trade as they will, presumably.
- **Relations with other factions:** Resented and feared by some settlers; **deliberately targeted**
  by the Empire's frame-up as the convenient enemy; an uneasy presence at the crossing.
- **Open threads:** Paige holds proof of their innocence in the raids. A bridge to them — through the
  truth she carries — is there to be built if she chooses.

## Inter-Faction Relations
*(Notable alliances, feuds, and tensions the player has learned about.)*
- The Empire treats the elf and dwarf nations of Acente as **independent rival powers**.
- **The Empire is covertly turning settler against Wendfolk** at the crossing — staging false
  raids (settlers paid in imperial coin, kills marked to read as Wendfolk work) to fracture the two
  and frighten Selvecross into welcoming an imperial garrison. *Paige knows this; the town does not.*
- **Selvecross settlers vs. the Wendfolk:** an existing wariness the Empire's frame-up was designed
  to inflame into open enmity.
