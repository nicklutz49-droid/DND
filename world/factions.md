# Factions & Reputation

> Every faction the player knows of, the player's **standing** with each, and the shifting
> **relationships** between them. The chronicler updates standings as they change in play;
> the world-simulator moves factions between sessions. Only what the player actually knows
> belongs here — secret agendas live in `dm-secrets/hidden-npcs.md`, the Empire's true
> design in `dm-secrets/empire-metaplot.md`.

## Standing Scale
A faction's disposition toward the player sits on this band (always note *why* it moved):

`Reviled · Hostile · Distrusted · Neutral / Unknown · Accepted · Friendly · Trusted · Honored · Revered`

> A newcomer starts at **Neutral / Unknown** with nearly everyone.

## The Three Powers (frontier-wide)
The broad forces in motion — **not monoliths.** Specific tribes, imperial offices, and
settler companies get their own blocks below as the player meets them. (Full detail in
`world/world-bible.md` §4.)

### The Empire of Acente
- **What it is:** The human-founded imperial state — bureaucracy, legions, magistrates,
  roads, taxes — expanding to absorb the frontier.
- **Player standing:** Neutral / Unknown
- **Notes:** Licenses and controls magic; venerates an imperial pantheon; treats the elf &
  dwarf nations of Acente as independent rivals.

### The Native Peoples
- **What it is:** The frontier's first peoples — great nations (goblin kingdoms, dragonborn
  city-states) and countless decentralized tribes of many kinds. They feud, ally, and trade
  among themselves.
- **Player standing:** Neutral / Unknown
- **Notes:** Each specific tribe/nation tracked separately as met.

### The Rival Settlers
- **What it is:** Independent colonists, chartered companies, and opportunists racing to
  stake claims before the Empire swallows the frontier.
- **Player standing:** Neutral / Unknown
- **Notes:** Specific companies and settlements tracked separately as met.
