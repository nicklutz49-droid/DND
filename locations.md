# Attributes

This is the structured backbone of a character. Six **core attributes** describe raw
capacity; one minor stat, **Luck**, is a small thumb on the scale. Skills
(`skills.md`) layer on top to describe *trained* ability.

The DM uses these in adjudication: an attribute sets the **ceiling** of raw talent, a
skill describes how **trained** the character is, and the plan + situation decide the
rest (`resolution.md`).

---

## The Scale

Mortal scores run **1–30**. **10 is the average healthy adult.** Most ordinary people
sit between 8 and 12 in everything. Specialists and the gifted climb higher.

| Band | Score | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Deficient | 1–3 | Profoundly impaired. Barely functional in this respect. |
| Poor | 4–7 | Clearly below average; a known weakness. |
| Low-average | 8–9 | Slightly under the norm. |
| **Average** | **10** | **The typical adult. The baseline of "a person."** |
| Capable | 11–13 | Above average — the fit, the sharp, the charming. Noticeable. |
| Gifted | 14–16 | Top ~5–10%. The best in a village; a natural talent. |
| Exceptional | 17–19 | The best in a city. Professional elite. People seek them out. |
| Renowned | 20–22 | Spoken of across a region. A reputation precedes them. |
| Legendary | 23–25 | Once in a generation. Songs get written. |
| Mythic | 26–29 | The outer edge of what flesh can do. |
| Mortal Limit | 30 | The ceiling of unaided mortal capacity. |
| Transcendent | 31+ | **Beyond mortal.** Reachable only through magic, transformation, or impossible decades of dedication — **late-campaign territory.** |

> A newcomer "competent but unproven" typically tops out around **14–16** in their best
> attributes at creation (see `character/CHARACTER-CREATION.md`). Growth across years of
> play is how you reach the higher bands.

## The Six Core Attributes

For each, here is what representative scores actually *look like*.

### Strength (STR) — physical power, force, carrying, melee impact
- **6** — Struggles with a full pack; loses most arm-wrestling.
- **10** — An ordinary adult. Can do a hard day's labor and feel it.
- **14** — A blacksmith or seasoned soldier. Carries the wounded out without slowing.
- **18** — Town strongman. Bends bars, breaks doors, fells a foe with one blow.
- **22** — Region-famous might. Lifts what two strong men can't.
- **26** — Heroic. Holds a portcullis, hurls a man across a room.
- **30** — The strongest mortal alive; deeds become legend.

### Dexterity (DEX) — agility, balance, precision, reflexes, fine motor control
- **6** — Clumsy; drops things, trips on stairs.
- **10** — Steady hands, ordinary footwork.
- **14** — A capable duelist or thief; quick and sure.
- **18** — A master archer or acrobat. Threads a needle on horseback.
- **22** — Moves like water; few can lay a hand on them.
- **26** — Catches arrows, dances across rooftops.
- **30** — Inhumanly precise and swift for a mortal.

### Constitution (CON) — endurance, health, resilience, pain tolerance
- **6** — Sickly; tires fast, slow to heal.
- **10** — Normal stamina and health.
- **14** — Marches all day; shrugs off a long winter.
- **18** — Iron-gutted; fights on through wounds that would drop others.
- **22** — Famously unbreakable; survives what should kill.
- **26** — Endures poison, plague, and exposure that fells armies.
- **30** — Refuses to die; a living legend of toughness.

### Intellect (INT) — reasoning, memory, knowledge, learning, deduction
- **6** — Slow to grasp the unfamiliar; struggles with letters.
- **10** — Practical sense; learns at a normal pace.
- **14** — Sharp. A scholar, tactician, or skilled tradesman's mind.
- **18** — Brilliant. Reads situations others miss; learned in many fields.
- **22** — A renowned mind; advisors and mages seek their counsel.
- **26** — Genius of the age; reshapes how people understand the world.
- **30** — The most brilliant mortal mind alive.

### Charisma (CHA) — presence, persuasion, leadership, force of personality
- **6** — Off-putting or invisible; people tune them out.
- **10** — Gets along fine; unremarkable presence.
- **14** — Magnetic in a room; a natural negotiator or leader of a few.
- **18** — Commands crowds; inspires loyalty; talks past hostile guards.
- **22** — A leader of movements; people follow them to danger.
- **26** — A figure people would die for; reshapes loyalties with a speech.
- **30** — Magnetism that founds nations and bends kings.

### Willpower (WIL) — resolve, courage, focus, mental fortitude, resistance to magic & fear
- **6** — Easily cowed, rattled, or manipulated.
- **10** — Holds their nerve in ordinary trouble.
- **14** — Steady under fire; hard to frighten or fool.
- **18** — Unshakeable. Resists pain, terror, and mental magic.
- **22** — A will of iron that bends others' resolve.
- **26** — Cannot be broken by fear, torture, or sorcery short of the greatest powers.
- **30** — An indomitable spirit; legends of unbreakable will.

## Luck (LCK) — the minor stat

Rated **1–10** (separate from the core scale; **3–5 is typical**). Luck is the DM's
**tiebreaker and occasional intervention** — used *sparingly*. It can tip a coin-flip
moment, soften a disaster into a mere setback, or let a long shot land. It should
**never override** a wildly implausible plan or a clear skill mismatch. Skills and traits
decide outcomes; Luck only nudges the margins.

- **1–2** — Snakebit. The world seems to conspire against them.
- **3–5** — Ordinary fortune.
- **6–8** — Fortune's favorite; the breaks tend to fall their way.
- **9–10** — Uncanny luck; survives the unsurvivable a touch too often to be coincidence.

---

## How attributes change

Attributes rise slowly and meaningfully through play — relentless training, hardship
survived, mentorship, magical means, or pivotal story milestones. A point gained should
feel *earned* across sessions, not handed out. The chronicler records any change with
its in-game cause and date. Crossing into **Transcendent (31+)** is a late-campaign,
narratively momentous event, never routine.
