# Skills & Proficiencies

Where **attributes** are raw capacity, **skills** are *training*. The campaign is
**classless** — there are no fixed classes. The character is defined by the specific
skills they've trained and the abilities they've learned, and they **acquire new skills
and deepen old ones over the course of the campaign** through practice, teachers, and
hard experience.

In adjudication (`resolution.md`), a check leans on the **most relevant skill** paired
with its **governing attribute**. A trained Journeyman with a modest attribute often
beats a gifted amateur — training matters.

---

## Proficiency Tiers

Every skill sits at one of these tiers. They describe *what a character can credibly
attempt*, and how much weight the skill carries in the DM's judgment.

| Tier | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Untrained** | No formal training. Can attempt only the obvious; fumbles anything subtle. Relies on raw attribute alone. |
| **Novice** | Has the basics. Handles routine tasks; struggles under pressure or with complexity. |
| **Apprentice** | Competent. Reliable on ordinary work; can improvise within reason. |
| **Journeyman** | A working professional. Trusted with real responsibility; handles hard tasks well. |
| **Expert** | Among the best in a region. Makes difficult work look easy; teaches others. |
| **Master** | Renowned. Attempts feats most consider impossible and often succeeds. |
| **Grandmaster** | Legendary, perhaps unmatched in the known world. Redefines the craft. |

> A new character starts with **one Journeyman skill** (their defining competence), a
> few Apprentice and Novice skills, and everything else Untrained. Tiers rise through
> dedicated practice and story — see `character/CHARACTER-CREATION.md`.

## Skill List

Not exhaustive — the DM and player can add skills as the world demands. Each skill notes
its usual **governing attribute(s)**; the DM picks whichever fits the moment.

### Combat
- **Blades** (DEX/STR) — daggers, swords, axes in the hand.
- **Bows & Slings** (DEX) — ranged muscle weapons.
- **Polearms & Hafted** (STR/DEX) — spears, halberds, mauls.
- **Brawling** (STR/DEX) — fists, grapples, dirty fighting.
- **Thrown** (DEX) — knives, javelins, axes through the air.
- **Powder-arms** (DEX/WIL) — rare, temperamental gunpowder weapons; see `world-bible.md`.
- **Shields & Defense** (STR/CON) — guarding, footwork, soaking blows.

### Physical & Wilderness
- **Athletics** (STR/CON) — climbing, swimming, jumping, raw exertion.
- **Acrobatics** (DEX) — tumbling, balance, escaping bonds.
- **Stealth** (DEX) — moving unseen and unheard.
- **Survival** (CON/INT) — foraging, shelter, weather, tracking.
- **Nature** (INT) — flora, fauna, the land's own lore and dangers.
- **Riding & Handling** (DEX/CHA) — mounts and beasts.
- **Sleight of Hand** (DEX) — picking pockets and locks, palming.

### Knowledge
- **History** (INT) — peoples, wars, old powers, who owes whom.
- **Lore & Arcana** (INT) — magic, spirits, the strange and forbidden.
- **Medicine** (INT) — wounds, illness, surgery, herb-craft.
- **Engineering** (INT) — building, siege-works, machines, fortification.
- **Streetwise** (INT/CHA) — reading a town, finding the right shady person.
- **Commerce** (INT/CHA) — trade, prices, contracts, running a business.

### Social
- **Persuasion** (CHA) — reason, charm, appeal.
- **Deception** (CHA) — lies, disguise, misdirection.
- **Intimidation** (CHA/STR) — threats, presence, menace.
- **Insight** (INT/WIL) — reading people, sensing lies and intent.
- **Leadership** (CHA/WIL) — commanding, inspiring, organizing followers.
- **Performance** (CHA) — music, oratory, art, courtly grace.

### Craft & Tools
- **Smithing** (STR/INT), **Alchemy** (INT), **Cooking & Brewing** (INT),
  **Carpentry & Masonry** (STR/INT), **Leather & Textiles** (DEX), **Cartography** (INT),
  and other trades as needed. (Governing attribute in parentheses.)

### Languages & Tools (tracked, not tiered)
- **Languages** — each known tongue listed individually (spoken/written).
- **Tool proficiencies** — specific kits the character is trained with
  (lockpicks, healer's kit, navigator's tools, etc.).

## Magic (a discoverable skill family)

Magic is **rare, learned, and not a starting default.** No one begins as a sorcerer.
It is acquired in play — found, taught, bargained for, or awakened — and once learned,
each tradition behaves like its own skill that climbs the proficiency tiers (e.g.
*Hedge-witchery*, *Rune-lore*, *Spirit-binding* — forms emerge through the story). Keep
it understated: subtle, costly, and quietly powerful rather than flashy. The DM tracks
known magical abilities on the character sheet like any other skill, and gates new ones
behind discovery (`dm-secrets/`).

**Faith is one such doorway.** A priest's blessings draw on the *same* skill-family as any
other magic — reached through devotion rather than study, but mechanically identical. Divine
power is never a separate system, and it never proves the god is real (see
`world/world-bible.md` §3).
