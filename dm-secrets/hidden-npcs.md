# Hidden NPCs & True Agendas — DM ONLY

> Figures the player hasn't met, and the **secret agendas** of NPCs they *have* met
> (the public `world/npcs.md` holds only what the player knows). Promote revealed truths
> to the public file as the player learns them.

## Hidden Figures
*(Unmet NPCs of importance — Empire's hidden hand, tribal seers, rival masterminds.)*

### "The Advisor" — HIDDEN *(unmet; seeded Session 1)*
- **Who they really are:** The **colder imperial agent / inner-circle handler** who arrived on the frontier
  early and runs the Empire's quiet work at the crossing from the shadows. Identity, race, location, and
  cover are **to develop** — but they are the hinge the player keeps brushing without seeing.
- **True agenda:** Runs **both** local limbs of the Empire's design and keeps them walled off from each
  other on paper: **(1)** Verrane's **threshold-cataloguing** (Stage 1 — Verrane sends sealed reports to
  the advisor and flinches at the word), and **(2)** the **false-flag raid operation** (Stage 2 — Calder
  reported "only to the advisor"; the orders kept "the survey office" deliberately in the dark).
- **Mark:** A wax sigil — **a closed eye over a vertical line.** Let it recur as the breadcrumb that the
  advisor is behind a thing.
- **Connection to the metaplot:** The local face of the inner circle; the link the player must eventually
  trace to understand the survey and the raids are **one operation.** Paige holds documents that name the
  advisor and bear the sigil — without yet knowing this is also Verrane's superior.
- **How/when they might enter the story:** First as a recurring *absence* — a name, a seal, a chain of
  command — then as a person when the player traces the raids or the survey-reports far enough up.

### "R." — the Selvecross informer — HIDDEN *(unmet; identity unknown to player)*
- **Who they really are:** A **town informer with access to (or inside) the Selvecross wharfmaster's
  records**, who leaked **barge sailing-schedules** to the raiders for pay. **Initial: R. Drinks at the
  Drowned Crow** ("— to R., at the Crow, same as before"). True name, role, and motive **to develop** —
  candidates: a wharf clerk, a Council toady, one of Brennar's people, or a Crow regular.
- **True agenda:** Money, most likely — or leverage/coercion. Whether R. knew the "raids" were an imperial
  frame or thought they were ordinary brigands is **open** (a good reveal either way).
- **Connection to the metaplot:** The inside thread that made the false-flag op work; the loose end Paige
  can pull at the Drowned Crow. Exposing R. cracks the local operation further — and may point upward
  toward the advisor.
- **How/when they might enter the story:** Through the Crow — Mother Sable's gossip, watching who answers
  to "R.," matching the informer-slip's hand. A near-term hook off Paige's evidence.

## Secret Agendas of Known NPCs
*(For NPCs in `world/npcs.md` who are hiding something. NPCs introduced in the Selvecross
entry of `world/locations.md`; their public faces live there, their secrets here.)*

### Coll Verrane (imperial surveyor, Selvecross) — what the player doesn't know
- **The secret:** Verrane's charter is a cover. He maps and copies ruins not for the survey office
  but for an **inner-circle imperial commission** quietly cataloguing **elder thresholds** across the
  frontier (`empire-metaplot.md` Stage 1; the Sunken Gate is one target — see `hidden-locations.md`).
  He is a **mid-level instrument**, not a mastermind: he knows he is to find marked stone, copy the
  marks, and report up a chain he doesn't fully see; he half-suspects he's being used and is nervous
  about it. He is genuinely interested in scholars because he **cannot read the elder marks himself**
  and his superiors want them read.
- **His handler / above him:** *(to develop)* — a colder agent or "advisor" who arrived early and to
  whom Verrane sends sealed reports down the Selve. A natural Stage-1/2 reveal.
- **What betrays it / how it surfaces:** He overpays for rubbings of *threshold/warding* marks and
  loses interest the moment a ruin proves to be a mere dwelling or grave. He has sealed letters he
  won't discuss and flinches at the word "advisor." A scholar who actually translates the marks may
  realize Verrane doesn't want them *studied* so much as **located and reported** — and that the
  questions in his charter (if she ever reads it) match no ordinary survey.
- **SESSION 1 — what changed:** Paige worked the Drowned Stair job and **already suspects his survey is a
  cover for something else** (told the table as much). She fed him a **false, partial reading** so the
  seal would not loosen; he believes he has a genuine partial and **does not know she returned alone to
  make a pact with what's bound there.** He paid her triple to be rid of a "possession"-haunted site.
  **Critical secret the player has NOT connected:** Verrane's superior is **"the advisor"** — the *same*
  handler behind the raid frame-up Paige just exposed. The survey and the raids are two limbs of one body;
  Paige has documents naming the advisor but has not linked them to Verrane.

### Hald Brennar (town boss, Selvecross) — what the player doesn't know
- **The secret:** Brennar is no imperial man and despises the charter on principle — but he has
  quietly begun **taking the Empire's coin** to let Verrane's people come and go without Council
  notice, and to look the other way at what moves up the Wend. He tells himself it's just tariff and
  pragmatism; he doesn't grasp he's an early thread in the Empire's *Divide/Seed* play, trading the
  crossing's independence for short money. He'd be furious — and exposed before the Council — if it
  came out.
- **What betrays it / how it surfaces:** Imperial coin in settler hands; Verrane's odd freedom of
  movement; a bargeman or Council rival who's noticed and wants leverage. Good early work for a sharp
  newcomer — and a wedge the player could use against Brennar, or sell to him to bury.
- **SESSION 1 — tension to play:** Brennar **backed the raider bounty** and now wants the prisoners and
  the truth — but the truth Paige carries (imperial coin, an imperial frame-up, a *town informer*) sits
  uncomfortably close to **Brennar's own quiet dealings with the Empire.** He genuinely did not order the
  raids and would be alarmed by them; but if Paige flashes imperial coin and talk of "advisors," she risks
  brushing his secret. Whether he's an ally against the frame-up or a man with reasons to bury parts of it
  is the live question of the dock meeting.

### Calder (captured raider boss, Selvecross) — true backstory
- **The truth:** An **ex-imperial officer** turned the Empire's deniable instrument — hired to run the
  **false-flag raid operation** at the crossing: a crew of settlers-in-disguise, staging river-raids,
  marking kills to read as **Wendfolk** work, **paid for the outcome** (the balance due "when the Council
  sends to the river-fort for soldiers"). He **reported only to "the advisor"** and was ordered to keep the
  *survey office* out of it. The fresh, tally-strung imperial coin in his strongbox was his **wage**, not
  loot.
- **What he knows / is worth:** A live thread back to the advisor and the operation. He likely knows the
  advisor's drop, the informer R.'s identity or contact pattern, the next targets, and that the survey
  office was deliberately walled off. **Leverage, witness, or liability** depending on what Paige does with
  him. He is also a witness to **Paige's outlawed earth-magic.**
- **Status:** Alive, wounded (cracked ribs, split lip), **Paige's prisoner at the docks** as Session 1 ends.
