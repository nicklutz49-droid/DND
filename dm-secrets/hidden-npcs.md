### Coll Verrane (imperial surveyor, Selvecross) — what the player doesn't know
- **The secret:** Verrane's charter is a cover. He maps and copies ruins not for the survey office
  but for an **inner-circle imperial commission** quietly cataloguing **elder thresholds** across the
  frontier (`empire-metaplot.md` Stage 1; the Sunken Gate is one target — see `hidden-locations.md`).
  He is a **mid-level instrument**, not a mastermind: he knows he is to find marked stone, copy the
  marks, and report up a chain he doesn't fully see; he half-suspects he's being used and is nervous
  about it. He is genuinely interested in scholars because he **cannot read the elder marks himself**
  and his superiors want them read.
- **His handler / above him:** *(to develop)* — a colder agent or "advisor" who arrived early and to
  whom Verrane sends sealed reports down the Selve. A natural Stage-1/2 reveal.
- **What betrays it / how it surfaces:** He overpays for rubbings of *threshold/warding* marks and
  loses interest the moment a ruin proves to be a mere dwelling or grave. He has sealed letters he
  won't discuss and flinches at the word "advisor." A scholar who actually translates the marks may
  realize Verrane doesn't want them *studied* so much as **located and reported** — and that the
  questions in his charter (if she ever reads it) match no ordinary survey.

### Hald Brennar (town boss, Selvecross) — what the player doesn't know
- **The secret:** Brennar is no imperial man and despises the charter on principle — but he has
  quietly begun **taking the Empire's coin** to let Verrane's people come and go without Council
  notice, and to look the other way at what moves up the Wend. He tells himself it's just tariff and
  pragmatism; he doesn't grasp he's an early thread in the Empire's *Divide/Seed* play, trading the
  crossing's independence for short money. He'd be furious — and exposed before the Council — if it
  came out.
- **What betrays it / how it surfaces:** Imperial coin in settler hands; Verrane's odd freedom of
  movement; a bargeman or Council rival who's noticed and wants leverage. Good early work for a sharp
  newcomer — and a wedge the player could use against Brennar, or sell to him to bury.
