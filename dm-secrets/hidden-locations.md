### The Sunken Gate (locally "the Drowned Steps" / "the Drowned Stair") — PARTLY DISCOVERED (IR 1187, 6th–8th of Highsun)
> **STATUS:** Paige found, read, and bargained at this site in Session 1. Its **public facts are now
> promoted** to `world/locations.md` (location; that it is a warded **threshold**; that it holds a sealed
> **sentient entity**; that it is a "thin/dangerous place"; that the Pact was made there). **STILL HIDDEN
> below:** that this is an **imperial-catalogue target** of the inner circle's threshold-hunt, the metaplot
> tie, and *which* Near World it binds against. Do not expose the metaplot-target status. See also
> `dm-secrets/the-pact.md` for what the entity truly is and intends.
- **Type & nature:** A half-submerged elder ruin — a broad flight of black, mortarless stone steps
  descending *into* a hillside tarn, about **a day and a half up the Wend** from Selvecross, then a
  short way off the water into a boggy fold of the hills. Above the waterline the visible courses are
  carved with the elder people's marks; the **gate itself lies below the surface**, drowned when the
  tarn rose. Trappers know the steps exist and avoid them — the tarn "drinks" things and the water is
  unnaturally still — but no one has read the marks or grasped what they are.
- **Where:** Up the Wend ~1.5 days by canoe (settler-paced) into hill country; leave the water at a
  beaver-fouled side-creek and walk in to the tarn. Findable but easy to pass; the stair is mostly
  underwater and screened by alders.
- **Secret significance:** *(ties to `empire-metaplot.md` Stage 1 and `hidden-lore.md`.)* This is no
  tomb or vault — it is a **threshold**, a worked **thin place** where the elder people deliberately
  built against one of the Near Worlds (DM: decide which — spirit, dead, or fey — when it matters).
  The marks are not decoration but a **binding/ward**, and the rising tarn has been slowly drowning
  it for centuries. **This is one of the sites Coll Verrane was sent to find.** The Empire's inner
  circle isn't after fur or farmland — it is quietly **cataloguing elder thresholds across the
  frontier**, hunting the one that leads to what they truly seek (`hidden-lore.md` › *the land's old
  power*). Verrane himself likely doesn't know the full design; he knows only his charter: *find the
  marked stone, copy the marks, report.* What stirs in the drowned tarn if the wards are read aloud,
  disturbed, or broken is **for the DM to develop** — a first taste that the elder ruins are not
  inert.
- **How the player might discover it:** Several converging threads, suited to a scholar:
  - **Verrane's commission.** If Paige takes the surveyor's copyist/guide work, he points her at "old
    stone up the Wend" — and the Drowned Steps are the nearest marked site. He wants rubbings of the
    marks; he is vaguer than he should be about *why*.
  - **River talk.** Mother Sable or old trappers at the Drowned Crow will, if asked about the ruins,
    mention the steps that "go down into the still water" up the Wend, and that animals won't drink
    there.
  - **Her own eye.** A scholar reading even a fragment of the elder marks (on a trade-token, a trapper's
    luck-charm, a stone Verrane shows her) may recognize them as **warding/threshold script, not
    grave-marks** — and want to see the source. That instinct is the hook: the marks *bind* something.
