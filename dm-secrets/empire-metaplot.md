# The Empire's Design (Slow-Burn Metaplot) — DM ONLY

> The spine of the campaign. It moves **in the background** whether or not the player
> engages it; the player can push against it, exploit it, ignore it, or eventually try
> to break it. **Not everything connects to this** — let local stories breathe — but the
> pressure of the Empire's advance should be felt across the years.
>
> Seed below — **develop and revise it to fit your table.** Reveal only through play.

## The Surface Story (what settlers believe)
The Empire is expanding to civilize the frontier: roads, law, protection from the tribes,
markets for goods. Surveyors map; soldiers garrison; magistrates arrive. Ordinary,
imperial, inevitable.

## The Real Design (the secret)
*(Pick / blend / rewrite — the truth the player slowly uncovers. Suggested seed:)*
The frontier campaign is not really about land or taxes. The Empire's inner circle is
here for **something buried in the frontier** — an old power, site, or knowledge from the
land's hidden history (tie to `hidden-lore.md`). The settlements, the tribal wars, even
the conflicts among settlers are being **quietly steered** to clear the way to it and to
keep rivals distracted. The Empire would rather the frontier bleed itself than look up
and notice what's truly being taken.

## Hidden Stages (escalating; advance via world-simulator over months/years)
1. **Survey & Seed.** Imperial agents map the land and plant assets — trading posts,
   "advisors," debts owed. A few disappearances no one connects yet.
2. **Divide.** The Empire covertly fuels conflict (settler vs. tribe, tribe vs. tribe) to
   keep the frontier fractured and dependent. Clues: too-convenient raids, foreign coin.
3. **Consolidate.** Open garrisons, law, conscription. The free window is closing;
   independent settlers and tribes feel the squeeze.
4. **Reveal.** The true objective nears. Whatever the Empire sought becomes undeniable —
   and whoever holds it holds the frontier's future.
5. **Reckoning.** Open break. The player, by now a real power, faces the Empire on terms
   they helped shape — resistance, bargain, replacement, or their own nation.

## The Player's Leverage
Every settlement grown, faction won, secret uncovered, and alliance forged is a counter
against this clock. The metaplot is the world's intention; the player's choices decide
whether it succeeds.

## Threads to Drop as Hooks (early, low-key)
- A surveyor asking oddly specific questions about old ruins, not good farmland.
- Imperial coin turning up in a tribal raiders' camp.
- A settler charter with strange clauses no one can explain.
- An "advisor" who arrived before the settlers did.
