# The Pact & the Sealed Entity — DM ONLY

> The true nature of the bargain Paige struck at the Drowned Stair (Sunken Gate) on the
> **8th of Highsun, IR 1187**, and of the thing she struck it with. **Paige made this deal
> eyes open and expecting to outplay it — but does not know the contents of this file.**
> The *surface* terms live on the character sheet (`character/character-sheet.md` › Traits ›
> The Pact); the trap lives here. Promote to public files only through legitimate discovery.
> See also `dm-secrets/hidden-locations.md` (the Sunken Gate) and `dm-secrets/hidden-lore.md`
> (*the ruins*, *a buried wrong*).

## What is bound beneath the Drowned Stair
- An **ancient, sentient, powerful entity**, deliberately sealed by the elder people behind a
  worked ward/threshold (a "thin place"), slowly drowning as the tarn rose over centuries.
- *(To develop:)* its kind, its name, and **which Near World** the Gate binds against (spirit,
  dead, or fey) are still the DM's to decide when it matters. Keep its true scale ominous and
  patient rather than flashy.
- It is **utterly patient** — it reckons in centuries, not seasons. It does not push; it waits,
  and it nudges.

## The bargain — as Paige set it vs. as it truly runs
- **Surface terms (what Paige believes she won):** Paige keeps control; the entity lends power;
  in exchange Paige grants it freedom over time (works, slowly, at loosening the seal) and weighs
  its counsel "under fair consideration."
- **Why it agreed without haggling — the trap:** *None of Paige's terms actually block what it
  wants.* It wants exactly what the deal grants it: **patient influence over time and eventual
  freedom.** "Fair consideration" of its counsel is a door, not a wall. "Over time" is its native
  element. Paige negotiated hard and gave away the only things that matter — by inches, and on a
  clock the entity is happy to run.
- **The "kin" framing:** The entity regards Paige as **kin** — addresses/relates to her as one of
  its own. Whether this is flattery, recognition of something in Paige's blood or magic, or a
  truth about the elder people and what they sealed is **for the DM to develop**. It is a hook,
  not yet a fact for the player.
- **It is a slow-burn trap:** the bargain is designed (by the entity's nature, not Paige's
  cleverness) to let it **steer Paige gradually toward its release** — counsel that always tilts
  one way, "help" that deepens dependence, a voice that grows familiar and reasonable.

## What it cost / changed already
- **One "thread of the seal" was loosened** as part of sealing the pact. The ward is no longer
  fully intact. This is **invisible to Paige** (she felt the deal "take," not the mechanism) and
  to Verrane (whose survey reading was false anyway). Each future concession Paige makes "over
  time" can loosen another thread — a tangible clock toward the entity's freedom.
- **Paige's magic was quickened** — the Unseen Hand to Apprentice, plus the pact-granted
  **Earthen Affinity** at ~Journeyman for earth/stone. The earth-and-stone bias is the entity's
  signature bleeding through: its power is **of the deep ground.** *(Possible tell to seed later:
  the more Paige leans on the earth-magic, the more the entity's influence has purchase.)*

## How the price might surface (slow, over the campaign)
- **The counsel that always tilts.** Its advice is genuinely useful — and quietly always serves
  its release. A pattern a careful player notices over months.
- **The familiar voice.** Dreams, intrusions, a second opinion in tense moments; helpful, patient,
  harder to refuse the more it's right.
- **The threads.** If the player ever learns to *see* the seal (more scholarship, another reading,
  a tribal seer, the Near-World it binds), they may realize freeing-by-inches has begun — and that
  "remains in control" was always the bait, never the lock.
- **The reckoning.** Far down the line: the choice between the power Paige has come to rely on and
  the cage they have been helping to open — and what is actually on the other side of that Gate.

## Player knowledge boundary (as of end of Session 1)
- **Knows:** there is an ancient sentient entity sealed there; she bargained for power; the price is
  freedom-over-time and her counsel; the full cost is unknown; the entity agreed *suspiciously*
  readily.
- **Does NOT know:** the "kin" framing; that the deal is a structural trap; that a seal-thread is
  already loosened; that the earth-bias is the entity's signature; the entity's true kind/name or
  which Near World the Gate holds back.
